Kintsugi (ÈáëÁ∂ô„Åé) is the Japanese art of repairing broken pottery by mending the areas of breakage with powdered gold, silver, or platinum. It invokes the Japanese philosophy of wabi-sabi : perceiving the beauty in the imperfect, impermanent, and incomplete aspects of life and things.

We all have our flaws and imperfections, we all are a little broken. That's just fine. Kintsugi teaches us that our imperfections are the also part of the beauty on us, and with every mistake and hurt we grow stronger and wiser.

Kintsugi and philosophy

Kintsugi reminds us that sometimes things wil break, collapse, stop working, find its end...
But that is a normal aspect of life. We all carry our scars and traumas, and we all remember what we lost and what couldn't be restored.
We must cherish our imperfections, for they make us become what we are. We have to celebrate not only what we have, but also what was gone.

Like fine gold, we shine and glow with the beauty that results from when we have been broken.
Make amends with yourself. Understand that we are not meant to be perfect, and with each error and wrongdoing, we must also get one step closer to become better.
